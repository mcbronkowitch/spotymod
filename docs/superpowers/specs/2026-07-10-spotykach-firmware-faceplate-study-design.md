# Spotykach Firmware Faceplate Study

## Goal

Create a coherent set of website-ready concept images showing alternative ambient-sequencer firmware ideas on the existing Spotykach hardware. The main image must read as a firmware annotation study, not as a new hardware design.

The main image uses an unchanged official Spotykach photograph with a separate annotation layer. Freer generated images may appear only as clearly labeled speculative detail explorations.

## Hardware invariants

- Preserve the physical Spotykach enclosure and its proportions.
- Keep every existing knob, large encoder, toggle switch, touchpad, jack and LED in exactly the same position and at the same scale.
- Do not add screens, controls, sockets or other hardware.
- Only the faceplate graphics, labels and implied firmware mapping change.
- Use the official Spotykach product photography as a hardware and visual reference, not as an edit target.

## Shared visual direction

- Clean faceplate study with a frontal, slightly elevated product-view perspective.
- Black panel with warm cream/gold screen-printed graphics in the tactile, abstract Synthux collage language.
- Sparse petrol/teal accents connect the image to the application website without replacing the Spotykach identity.
- Warm orange LED rings and indicators.
- Light, neutral background compatible with the off-white application page.
- No hands, patch cables, surrounding instruments or studio clutter.
- Labels must be short, English, legible and limited to the controls that communicate the concept.
- Small study identifier: `SPOTYKACH / AMBIENT MODULATION FIRMWARE STUDY`.
- Primary delivery is a landscape website image with comfortable space around the instrument.

## Concept 1 - Modulation Matrix (style master)

The two mirrored hardware halves become two modulation engines, `MOD A` and `MOD B`.

- Large controls communicate `RATE` and `DEPTH` or `MOTION`.
- Smaller controls communicate `SHAPE`, `RANGE`, `PROBABILITY` and `SMOOTH`.
- Toggle states include `LOOP / EVOLVE`, `SYNC / FREE` and `STEP / FLOW`.
- The twelve existing touchpads form two target banks. Suggested targets are `PITCH`, `TIMBRE`, `DENSITY`, `SPACE`, `FEEDBACK` and `LEVEL`.
- The central area communicates interaction between the engines through `COUPLE`, `DRIFT` and `MORPH`.
- The result must make the statement "modulation is the instrument" immediately understandable.

## Concept 2 - Sequence versus Modulation

The same hardware becomes a polyrhythmic relationship between a musical sequence and an independent modulation cycle.

- One half represents a 16-step musical sequence.
- The other represents a 12-step modulation cycle.
- Faceplate graphics clearly communicate the `16 vs. 12` relationship without adding physical controls.
- Controls emphasize sequence stability, phase offset, probability, cycle length and controlled evolution.

## Concept 3 - Ambient Performance Surface

The mirrored halves remain readable as two playable sound or voice layers.

- Performance-oriented mappings emphasize `DENSITY`, `SPREAD`, `DRIFT`, `FEEDBACK`, `MOTION` and `SPACE`.
- Central controls communicate blending and interaction between both layers.
- The instrument should feel immediate and playable while retaining the same modulation-led identity.

## Production sequence

1. Use an official Spotykach photograph as an unchanged hardware layer.
2. Add the Concept 1 modulation logic as a visibly separate annotation overlay.
3. Inspect hardware fidelity, label readability, composition and visual match with the application website.
4. Present generated faceplate renders only as speculative detail explorations.
5. Derive Concepts 2 and 3 from the approved annotation language.

## Acceptance criteria

- Existing hardware geometry and control placement are visibly unchanged.
- Concept 1 clearly reads as two linked modulation engines.
- The faceplate feels related to Spotykach while remaining an original firmware study.
- The image fits the application website's light editorial design.
- Labels are restrained and sufficiently legible for a portfolio image.
- All three final concepts share one perspective, lighting system and graphic language.
