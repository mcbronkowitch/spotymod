# Bench run 2026-07-22 — `ef84678`

Measured on a Daisy Seed (STM32H750). 480000000 Hz core clock, block size 96, dcache+icache, `-ffast-math -funroll-loops`. Block budget 960000 cycles.

## Verdict

**2x4 budget — go/no-go.** The full instrument at its worst case (8 voices, COLOR 4-note on both parts, all FX on, high diffusion, echo at max) costs **98 % of the block budget offline**, and **98 % measured inside a real audio callback**. The anchored figure is the one that decides. **Conclusion: the 2x4 architecture fits.** The anchored figure is under 100 % of the block budget.

**Cost per candidate, relative to one real spotymod voice.**

- `modal_voice` — 6.1x one real voice (44x a bare oscillator kernel)
- `string_voice` — 2.0x one real voice (14x a bare oscillator kernel)
- `resonator` — 5.5x one real voice (40x a bare oscillator kernel)
- `formant_osc` — 0.30x one real voice (2.2x a bare oscillator kernel)
- `vosim_osc` — 0.93x one real voice (6.8x a bare oscillator kernel)
- `harmonic_osc` — 0.48x one real voice (3.5x a bare oscillator kernel)
- `grainlet_osc` — 0.65x one real voice (4.8x a bare oscillator kernel)
- `z_osc` — 0.99x one real voice (7.3x a bare oscillator kernel)
- `variable_shape_osc` — 0.18x one real voice (1.4x a bare oscillator kernel)

**SRAM vs SDRAM.** The grain-read proxy (8 scattered interpolated stereo reads per sample, identical window in both regions) costs **5.3x** in SDRAM against SRAM. That is a bare access pattern, written before the sampler existed to stand in for it; the `sampler_win_*` pair below is the same contrast with the real engine around it. The Oliverb pair reads **1.3x**, and the shortened echo-style streaming walk **3.3x**.

**The texture deck.** Both parts on the sampler at its worst case — DENS 8, SIZE 0.05 (128-sample grains spawning every 16), MOTION 1 scattering reads over the whole 42 s record buffer, SCAN running, and instrument_worst's FX chain unchanged around it — costs **92 % of the block budget on the mean block and 113 % on the worst**, against **93 % / 98 %** for the same box with both parts on the synth. The mean is the cheaper of the two; the peak is not. **Conclusion: the texture deck fits on average and overruns on peaks.** The grain count itself is already capped (`kSpawnHeadroom`) and the ablations say the remainder is not a burst: with the MOTION and SIZE lanes quiet the cloud's per-block work is flat to 1 %, and the worst block is simply the ceiling's worth of grains plus the whole FX chain. Dropping either FLUX or the reverb from this patch brings it under 100 %; capping grains harder does not.

Solo, the same cloud is **18 %** mean / **22 %** peak, a musical setting (DENS 4, half-second grains, MOTION 0.5, playhead parked) is **11 %** mean, and running an overdub underneath the worst-case cloud costs **1.1x** its mean. SCAN driven at the VCV host's rate — every 16 samples, six times the engine's own control tick, the `std::pow` `SamplerEngine::set_scan`'s comment flags — reads **1.0x** against the same cloud without it. With the whole engine around it the record buffer's region costs **1.2x** (`sampler_win_sdram` over `sampler_win_sram`, identical settings and identical 8192-frame content), well under the bare proxy's figure above: the scheduler, window, filter and normalization are all region-blind and dilute it.

*Figures in this section are quoted to whole percentage points and two significant figures for ratios — honest to what this bench can actually resolve (intra-run jitter of roughly 1700 cycles on a 1.5M-cycle workload, and a cross-build layout shift that moved a 29K-cycle workload by about 7%). The tables below retain full measured precision.*
